Job Roles And Responsibilities

  • Locomotive Engineer (Train Driver): Operates trains, ensuring safe and timely transportation. Follows signal indications and speed regulations.
  • Conductor: Manages passenger safety and comfort. Assists with ticket sales and checks tickets.
  • Signal Operator: Controls railway signals and track switches. Ensures trains are directed onto the correct tracks.
  • Track Maintenance Worker: Inspects and repairs tracks, signals, and related infrastructure. Ensures track safety and compliance with regulations.
  • Railway Station Manager: Oversees the daily operations of a railway station. Manages staff, handles passenger inquiries, and ensures station services run smoothly.
  • Train Dispatcher: Coordinates the movement of trains on the tracks. Ensures trains adhere to schedules and manages any delays or issues.
  • Railway Safety Officer: Monitors and ensures adherence to safety protocols. Conducts safety audits and investigations.
  • Customer Service Representative: Assists passengers with inquiries and complaints. Provides information on schedules, fares, and ticketing.

Required Skills

Technical Skills: Engineering Knowledge: For roles like railway engineers, understanding mechanical, electrical, and civil engineering principles is crucial. Maintenance and Repair: Skills in maintaining and repairing trains, tracks, and signaling systems are essential.
Operational Skills: Train Operation: Knowledge of train control systems and safe driving practices for train operators. Signaling and Communication: Skills in operating and interpreting signaling systems to ensure safe and efficient train operations.
Communication Skills: Effective communication with passengers, especially in roles like ticketing and customer support. Problem-Solving: Addressing passenger concerns and operational issues promptly and effectively.
IT Skills: Proficiency in computer systems and software for scheduling, ticketing, and operational management. Each role within the railway sector will have its own specific requirements, but these general skills are often applicable across various positions

Salary Expectations

Commercial railway Officer salary at Indian Railways India ranges between ₹6.5 Lakhs to ₹14.3 Lakhs.

Indian Railways Senior Officer salary in India ranges between ₹4.8 Lakhs to ₹15 Lakhs with an average annual salary of ₹9 Lakhs.

Railway Recruitment Boards (RRBs) decide RRB Group D salary ranges between INR ₹3.0 to ₹5.0 lakh per annum

The basic RRB NTPC salary for 12th pass posts ranges between ₹2.3Lakhs to ₹2.6 Lakhs (varies as per posts).
